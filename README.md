# 📊 Meesho Social Commerce Analytics (India)

## Overview

Meesho operates in a unique segment of Indian e-commerce where social selling, reseller-driven demand, high Cash-on-Delivery (COD) adoption, and significant Tier-2/Tier-3 market penetration shape customer behavior.

This project simulates a realistic Meesho-style social commerce ecosystem and analyzes it using SQL, Python, and Tableau to answer real business questions related to revenue growth, customer retention, churn risk, product performance, and supplier quality.

The objective was not to build dashboards first, but to approach the dataset from a business analyst's perspective by identifying important business problems and solving them through data.

---

## Business Problem

Social commerce platforms face challenges that differ from traditional e-commerce marketplaces:

* High dependence on Cash on Delivery (COD)
* Frequent product returns and cancellations
* Strong reliance on repeat purchases
* Supplier quality management
* Customer retention and churn
* Revenue concentration among a small customer base

This project investigates these areas using data-driven analysis and business metrics.

## Business Questions Answered

### Revenue Analytics

* What is the total GMV generated by the platform?
* What is the Average Order Value (AOV)?
* How does revenue change month over month?
* What is the platform's growth trend?

### Customer Analytics

* What percentage of customers are repeat buyers?
* Who are the highest-value customers?
* Which customers are at risk of churn?
* How is revenue distributed among customers?

### Retention Analytics

* What is the customer retention rate over time?
* How do customer cohorts behave after their first purchase?
* What does RFM segmentation reveal about customer loyalty?

### Product Analytics

* Which categories generate the highest revenue?
* Which products contribute most to sales?
* Which product combinations are frequently purchased together?

### Operations Analytics

* What percentage of orders are returned?
* What percentage of orders are cancelled?
* How dependent is the business on COD?

### Supplier Analytics

* Which suppliers have the highest return rates?
* Which suppliers may be contributing to quality issues?

## Key Findings

* Fashion emerged as the highest revenue-generating category.
* A relatively small group of customers contributed a significant share of total revenue.
* Customer retention declined steadily after the first purchase, highlighting the importance of re-engagement strategies.
* COD remained the dominant payment method, reflecting common behavior in Indian social commerce.
* Supplier-level return analysis identified vendors with disproportionately high return rates.
* Several product combinations showed strong cross-selling potential.
